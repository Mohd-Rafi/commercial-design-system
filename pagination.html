Pagination

Download More View Figma File

Pagination is a technique used in web design and user interfaces to break down a large set of content or data into smaller, more manageable chunks or pages. It enables users to navigate through the content by providing links, buttons, or other controls to move to the next or previous page.

In web applications or websites with extensive lists, tables, or search results, pagination is commonly used to improve usability and performance. Instead of loading and displaying all the content at once, the data is divided into logical sections or pages, typically containing a fixed number of items or entries.

LARGE PAGINATION

Pagination is used for changing larger page content items. It can be shown in steps or without steps.

The step-based display shows all available and directly selectable pages or content items as well as the current position. The current position is shown by means of underlining and a strong contrast.
